Blockchain-Based Decentralized Crowdfunding DApp
Group Project Distinction
Oct 2025 — Dec 2025  |  Hong Kong, Lingnan University
Problem
Traditional crowdfunding platforms suffer from opaque fund flows, high intermediary costs, and lack of donor trust. The team built a fully decentralized crowdfunding donation system on the Ethereum blockchain to eliminate these systemic trust issues using smart contract automation.
Data & Stack
On-chain data stored as Solidity state variables on Ethereum Sepolia Testnet and Hardhat local network. Frontend: Vite + React + Ethers.js + Tailwind CSS. Backend: 4 Solidity smart contracts (donation_person_item, donation_group_item, donator_contract, identity_store).
Approach
Layered architecture: UI layer ↔ business logic ↔ blockchain layer. Contracts encode fund-release rules, multi-sig group voting, and community-driven anti-fraud reporting. Security hardened via Foundry (Forge) automated testing; two critical vulnerabilities patched (withdrawal logic error + DoS cleanup vulnerability).
Outcome & Impact
Full system passed all regression tests (testRefund ✓, test_CleanupHistory ✓, test_MultiSig ✓). Deployed on Sepolia Testnet. Achieved Distinction grade. Demonstrated that blockchain can reduce donation fraud risk and improve fund transparency — verified by security audit.
My Contribution (Frontend Lead): Independently designed and implemented all four core frontend pages in React: CreateItem.jsx (contract deployment with type selection, parameter input, real-time time preview), DonationMain.jsx (donation interface with real-time status feedback), GroupVoting.jsx (multi-signature voting UI), and Report.jsx (anti-fraud reporting with ETH fee mechanism). Integrated all pages with Solidity smart contracts via Ethers.js, handled wallet connection logic, transaction state visualization, and error handling.

Gen-Z Students' Online Luxury Goods Purchasing Behavior Analysis
Individual Project Bachelor Thesis
Sep 2023 — May 2024  |  Shenzhen University
Problem
Understanding what drives Gen-Z university students to purchase luxury goods online — a consumer segment growing rapidly in China yet understudied quantitatively. The research aimed to identify key behavioral drivers and provide empirical evidence for both brand marketers and consumer education policymakers.
Data
Primary survey data — 260 valid questionnaires collected online from Gen-Z university students across China. Variables covered consumption attitude, purchase motivation (hedonic, status, self-expression), social influence, platform trust, and actual purchase behavior.
Approach
Full quantitative pipeline in SPSS: reliability analysis (Cronbach's α), validity testing (EFA/CFA), Pearson correlation analysis, and multiple regression analysis. Theoretical framework grounded in consumer behavior theory, Maslow's hierarchy, and TAM (Technology Acceptance Model).
Outcome & Impact
Validated that consumption attitude, social identity motivation, and platform trust are significant predictors of online luxury purchase intent among Gen-Z. Results provide evidence-based recommendations for luxury brands' digital marketing strategies and for guiding rational consumption education. Full thesis accepted by Shenzhen University.
Full Individual Ownership: End-to-end solo project — literature review, theoretical model construction, questionnaire design, online survey distribution and collection, all SPSS statistical analyses, result interpretation, and academic writing. Supervised by Prof. Li Tong (Professor, School of Management, Shenzhen University).
